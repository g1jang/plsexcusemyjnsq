Personal Content · 2020

Before It Was
a Strategy

During lockdown I started posting on TikTok with no real plan, no following, nothing. Just videos I thought were funny or felt timely. Some of them spread further than I expected. This isn't me claiming credit for anything groundbreaking. I hopped on sounds and memes like everyone else. I just happened to do it at the right moment with the right idea, and over a million people watched.

1.1M Combined Views
235K Combined Likes
11.6K Shares
0 Paid promotion

The video itself. Trending sound, right idea, right moment.

No paid promotion. No following. 2020.

The Read

  • A trending sound with a million other videos on it is not a strategy. Knowing which idea cuts through anyway is. Most people get that wrong and wonder why their version got 400 views.
  • The 863K video wasn't unique content. It was a meme format everyone was using. The difference was specificity — it spoke to a very particular person, and that person shared it with everyone they knew who was also that person.
  • Average watch time of 7 seconds sounds bad until you remember the video was probably 10 seconds long. People watched it more than once.
  • None of this was a brand play or a career move. Which is probably why it worked. There's something about posting with nothing to lose that reads as genuine, and audiences in 2020 especially could smell calculated from a mile away.
